Data is noise.
Until someone
needs an answer.

I got into data science for a simple reason. People have questions they cannot answer alone. Not because they lack intelligence. Because the signal is buried. I find the signal. That has always been the job.

The model is never the point. The person who needed the answer is the point. I build toward that person every time.

At Deakin University I earned a Master of Data Science. My mentors appreciated the work. My cohort noticed it. The High Distinctions were not the goal. They were what happened when the work was genuinely good.

Every project I ship is documented so completely that a stranger could pick it up tomorrow and carry it forward. That is not perfectionism. That is respect for the next person in the chain.

When I am not in the data I am out running. Same discipline. Different shoes. Both teach you that the work is done in the long miles nobody sees.

I don't measure success by the accuracy of the model.
I measure it by whether the person asking the question
got a better answer.
